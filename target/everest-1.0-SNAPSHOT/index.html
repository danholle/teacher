EVEREST
for mere mortals
After returning from the 1994 American North Ridge Everest Expedition, I captured a few thoughts in my long-way-from-Home-Page. The response was surprising and gratifying, with the visitor count numbering in 6 digits... at a time (over 20 years ago) when that was humblingly-big number.

I'm not a professional journalist, professional photographer, or professional climber. Chances are, you're not, either.

Instead of trying to depress you with dramatic accounts of tragedies, dazzle you with other-worldly images at strange angles, or glorify super-human feats of skill and endurance... this site (as the older one) tries to bring the Everest experience down to Earth a little bit. I'd like to give you a little insight into what it's like to be there.

To do that, let's pretend that it's May, 1994... and we're both on the north side of Everest, at 7000 metres (23,000 feet). I'll answer your questions, tell you stories, and show you some pictures...
